# Al Brooks Price Action Cheat Sheet

This reference guide summarizes key trading concepts from Al Brooks' Price Action methodology, customized for stock trend analysis.

---

## 1. Candlestick (Bar) Types

Every K-line bar is classified based on the relationship between its body (Open-Close) and its total range (High-Low).

### Trend Bars (趋势棒)
- **Definition**: The body occupies a significant portion of the total bar range (typically $\ge 50\%$).
- **Bull Trend Bar**: Close is near the High. Indicates buyers dominate.
- **Bear Trend Bar**: Close is near the Low. Indicates sellers dominate.
- **Significance**: Indicates momentum. Consecutive trend bars suggest a **Spike** (Breakout).

### Doji / Trading Range Bars (区间/十字星棒)
- **Definition**: The body is small relative to the total range (typically $< 30\%$).
- **Significance**: Indicates hesitation, balance, or lack of direction. A series of Dojis forms a **Trading Range** or tight trading range.

---

## 2. Market Structure & Trend Phases

Al Brooks divides market cycles into two primary phases: **Trend** and **Trading Range**.

### Trend Phase (趋势阶段)
- **Spike (Breakout)**: A rapid price movement represented by one or more strong trend bars. High urgency.
- **Channel**: A sloped corridor that follows a Spike. Price moves slower, with frequent pullbacks, but continues to make:
  - **Uptrend**: Higher Highs (HH) and Higher Lows (HL).
  - **Downtrend**: Lower Highs (LH) and Lower Lows (LL).

### Trading Range Phase (区间阶段)
- Price fluctuates horizontally between support and resistance.
- Highs and lows are often tested and fail (false breakouts are common, $>80\%$ fail).

---

## 3. The 20-Period EMA (20 EMA 均线)

Al Brooks uses the 20-period Exponential Moving Average (EMA) as the primary baseline.

- **Support/Resistance**: In a trend, the EMA 20 acts as dynamic support (in an uptrend) or resistance (in a downtrend).
- **EMA Gap Bar (缺口棒)**: 
  - **Bull Gap Bar**: A bar whose Low is completely above the EMA 20. Indicates extreme bullish momentum.
  - **Bear Gap Bar**: A bar whose High is completely below the EMA 20. Indicates extreme bearish momentum.
- **First Touch**: The first pullback to touch the EMA 20 after a long breakout is a high-probability trade location in the direction of the breakout.

---

## 4. Pullback Signal Counting (H1/H2 & L1/L2)

Pullback count helps traders identify when a correction is ending and the trend is about to resume.

### Bull Trend Pullback (买入设置)
1. **High 1 (H1)**: During a pullback in an uptrend, the first bar whose High is higher than the High of the prior bar.
2. **High 2 (H2)**: After an H1, if the price continues to pull back (making another lower high), the next bar whose High is higher than the High of the prior bar is an H2. 
   - *Note*: An H2 pullback is often a double bottom pullback and is a high-probability buy signal.

### Bear Trend Pullback (卖出设置)
1. **Low 1 (L1)**: During a pullback in a downtrend, the first bar whose Low is lower than the Low of the prior bar.
2. **Low 2 (L2)**: After an L1, if the price continues to pull back (making another higher low), the next bar whose Low is lower than the Low of the prior bar is an L2.
   - *Note*: An L2 pullback is a high-probability short entry.

---

## 5. Wedge Pullback (三推楔形拉回)

- **Definition**: A pullback consisting of three distinct legs (pushes) against the trend.
- **Wedge Bottom**: In an uptrend pullback, three pushes down (making 3 consecutive lower lows). This represents seller exhaustion and is a very strong reversal buy signal.
- **Wedge Top**: In a downtrend pullback, three pushes up (making 3 consecutive higher highs). This represents buyer exhaustion and is a strong reversal short signal.
